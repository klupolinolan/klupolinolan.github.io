With a growing number of households becoming food insecure on Long Island, is there a solution that can help solve an issue that hasn’t been at the front of Long Islanders minds?

WHAT IS A FOOD DESERT?

WHAT IS A FOOD DESERT?

According to the USDA, food deserts are defined as, “parts of the country vapid of fresh fruit, vegetables, and other healthful whole foods, usually found in impoverished areas. This is largely due to a lack of grocery stores, farmers’ markets, and healthy food providers.” Food deserts are often thought of as problems when it comes to urban areas, but they are also an issue when it comes to suburban areas. According to Feeding America in their 2016 Map The Meal Gap, there are 82,870, or 5.5%, food-insecure people in Suffolk County alone. In that same study, it found that 42,110, or 12.6%, of those people, are estimated to be children. Their website also states that 45% of those children fall above the poverty line and are eligible for federal nutrition programs.
